:- WattWatch :Analyzing Urban Energy Consumption for Smarter Cities :-

1. Energy Demand Concentration :-
-> Insight:

Residential consumers account for the highest total energy consumption
compared to Commercial and Industrial segments.

-> Business Meaning:

Residential demand is the primary load driver.

Demand-side management programs should prioritize households.

Smart metering and time-of-use pricing can reduce peak load stress.

2. Zone-Based Energy Hotspots :-
-> Insight:

Certain zones (e.g., North/Central if highest in your 
consume significantly more energy than others.

-> Business Meaning:

High-demand zones may need infrastructure upgrades.

Transformer capacity and grid reinforcement should focus on these areas.

Opportunity for rooftop solar or distributed generation.

3. Monthly Trend Shows Seasonal Peaks :-
-> Insight:

Energy consumption increases during specific months (likely summer or winter).

-> Business Meaning:

Demand is seasonal.

Utility company should prepare for peak capacity months.

Introduce seasonal tariff optimization.

4. Peak Usage Pattern :-
-> Insight:

High PeakUsage_kWh values indicate short-duration heavy loads.

-> Business Meaning:

Grid stress risk during peak hours.

Recommend smart load balancing.

Encourage staggered industrial shifts.

5. Outage Impact Analysis :-
-> Insight:

Some zones show higher outage minutes despite moderate consumption.

-> Business Meaning:

Infrastructure reliability issues.

Preventive maintenance required.

High outage zones affect customer satisfaction.

This is critical for business case study.

6. Revenue Concentration :-

-> Using:
EnergyConsumed × TariffRate

-> Insight:

Commercial/Industrial sectors may generate higher revenue despite lower total consumption.

-> Business Meaning:

These segments are high-value customers.

Service reliability is financially critical.

Tailored pricing strategies can increase profitability.

7. Low Energy Efficiency Zones :-

-> Using:
High Consumption + High Outage Minutes

-> Insight:

Some zones consume heavily but also suffer outages → indicates inefficiency.

-> Business Meaning:

Energy loss or distribution inefficiency.

Possible outdated infrastructure.

Investment priority areas.

8. Weekday vs Weekend Pattern :-
-> Insight:

Commercial consumption drops on weekends while residential increases.

-> Business Meaning:

Predictable demand shifts.

Smart grid scheduling can optimize supply.

Industrial contracts can include demand flexibility clauses.

9. Meter Health Monitoring :-

-> Using:
MeterStatus + OutageMinutes

-> Insight:

Faulty meters correlate with higher outage reporting.

-> Business Meaning:

Replace faulty meters.

Reduce technical losses.

Improve billing accuracy.
